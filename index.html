Pool Cleaning FAQ

Answers to common questions about weekly pool service, water care, and keeping your pool swim-ready.

What is included in your regular pool cleaning service?

Regular pool cleaning typically includes skimming debris, brushing surfaces, emptying baskets, checking equipment, and testing and balancing the water so your pool stays clean and ready to use.

How often should my pool be cleaned?

Most homeowners do best with weekly pool cleaning. In Florida, regular service helps keep up with heat, rain, debris, algae risk, and water chemistry changes before they become bigger problems.

Do you help with green pools or cloudy water?

Yes. If your pool has turned green or cloudy, a cleanup service can restore water clarity, remove buildup, and get the chemistry back where it needs to be.

Do I need to be home during pool service?

In most cases, no. As long as the service team has access to the pool area and equipment, routine cleaning and water treatment can usually be completed without you needing to be there.

Why is professional water balancing important?

Proper water chemistry helps protect swimmers, keeps the water looking clear, and reduces wear on surfaces and equipment. Consistent testing and balancing can prevent expensive problems later.
